The Eye of God

Helix Nebula (NGC 7293)

Story Behind the Shot

"This image was a test of patience. We had unusually high humidity for three consecutive nights, causing the guider to struggle with the faint guide stars. I had to throw away nearly 30% of the subs. However, on the final night, the seeing stabilized to sub-arcsecond levels, allowing me to resolve the fine cometary knots in the inner ring."

Technical Details

Optics Celestron C11
Integration 18.5 Hours
Camera ASI2600MM
Soft PixInsight
Mount EQ6-R Pro
Location Bortle 2

Scientific Intelligence

Located 650 light-years away in Aquarius, the Helix is a planetary nebula created at the end of a Sun-like star's life. The outer gases are being ionized by the exposed hot stellar core, or white dwarf.
